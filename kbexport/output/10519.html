Immunization dose schedule for VaxTrax

10519

Introduction

This article describes the configuration and updating of the c_immunization_dose_schedule table. 

Symptom

Cause

Workaround

Resolution

The c_immunization_dose_schedule table is automatically populated with EncounterPRO upgrades.  As configured by JMJ Technologies, Inc., the scheduling rules set forth in this table comply with the 2006 standard set forth by the Center for Disease Control (CDC).  See http://www.cdc.gov/nip/recs/child-schedule.htm.

Values in this table can be manually reconfigured.  They can also be deleted to allow the user to create his or her own vaccination rules through the user interface.  (This is not recommended as it may result in non-compliance with CDC standards.)

The columns of the c_immunization_dose_schedule table are as follows:

disease_id      The disease id is defined in c_disease.  Each disease that is prevented by a given vaccine needs to be defined in terms of a disease id.  Currently, vaccines are scheduled for the following diseases:  Diptheria, Tetanus, Pertussis, HIB, Polio, HepA, HepB, Varicella, Measles, Mumps, Rubella, Influenza, Menningococcus, and Pneumococcus.

dose_schedule_sequence    Each disease id must be linked to one or more dose_schedule_sequence ids.  Dose_schedule_sequence numbers identify the dosing rule(s) for for a particular dose sequence within a series of doses.  For example, if there are two different dosing rules that might apply to the third dose of a DTaP, each of those two different rules will be identified by a unique dose_schedule_sequence id. 

dose_number   The dose_number specifies the dose sequence within a series of doses to which the dose_schedule_sequence id pertains.  For example, if there are two different dosing rules that might apply to the third dose of a DTaP, there will be two unique dose_schedule_sequence ids, each of which will be associated with a dose_number of "3."

patient_age_range_id    The patient_age_range_id is defined in the c_age_range and c_age_range_category tables.  The age_range_category "Immunization" and its constituent age_range_ids should be used in configuring the patient_age_range_id.  The patient_age_range_id specifies the age range during which the a particular shot (defined by a disease_id, dose_schedule_sequence, and dose_number) may be administered. 

first_dose_age_range_id    This id is derived from the "Immunization" age_range_category in the c_age_range table.  It defines the minimum age at which a particular shot (defined by a disease_id, dose_schedule_sequence, and dose_number) may be given. 

last_dose_age_range_id     This id is derived from the Immunization age_range_category in the c_age_range table.  It defines the maximum age at which a particular shot (defined by a disease_id, dose_schedule_sequence, and dose_number) may be given. 

last_dose_interval_amount / last_dose_interval_unit_id     These two values define the minimum amount of time that should elapse between the time that the patient was given his or her most recent dose of a vaccine (defined by the dose number) and the next dose of the vaccine that is due.
