Introduction

JMJ Technologies maintains a master database of clinical content at its corporate location in Atlanta, Georgia.

A majority of this content corresponds the wide variety of picklists necessarily used in a structured data EHR.

In EncounterPRO’s case, this also includes workplans as well (these control automated workflow such as the sequencing of screens or performing automatic tasks)

This content is stored in relational database tables (MS SQL Server)

Periodically, as part of a general release or an upgrade, or sometimes in response to client customization activities, JMJ downloads this content from the master to local databases.

This download process is referred to as “syncing”

When a pick list is downloaded to a local site it is “owned” by JMJ unless the local site decides to make a local copy, which can then be customized at the local site.

Pick lists that are owned by JMJ can be overwritten by JMJ during the sync process.
