How to Maintain Records of HIPAA Disclosures

10535

Introduction

This article discusses how to maintain a record of HIPAA disclosures.  At present, it is anticipated that a HIPPA disclosure record would contain the following information:   The date of the disclosure; The name of the entity or person who received the protected health information; The address of such entity or person; A brief description of the information disclosed; A brief statement of the purpose of the disclosure.

Workaround

Resolution

There are several methods that could be used to capture information about HIPAA disclosures.  One way, would be to create a Patient Progress Note (with free text) from the Review Screen.

A second method would be to use the Document service that is new in EncounterPRO Version 5.0. 

The Document Service captures 1) an image of every document that is printed, faxed, or e-mailed 2) the identity of the person who has accomplished the printing, fax or e-mail, and 3) when delivery of the document is ordered as part of a treatment, the person to whom the document has been delivered.

In future enhancments, the document service will be enhanced to capture all of the information required by HIPAA even when a document is not ordered to be delivered as part of a treatment.
